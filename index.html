Essential Tips for Preventing Misbranding

Call Greeting Protocol

  1. Check the company identifier before answering
  2. Use the specific greeting script for that company:
    • Trieagle: "Thank you for calling Trieagle Energy, this is [name]. How may I provide reliable service today?"
    • TXU: "Welcome to TXU Energy, you're speaking with [name]. How can I assist with your energy needs today?"
    • Ambit: "Thank you for choosing Ambit Energy, my name is [name]. How may I help you save today?"
  3. Keep company-branded quick reference cards by your workstation

Workspace Management

  • Take a few seconds before answer the call to check the Brand
  • Close or minimize windows from other companies accounts
  • Apply company-specific call templates for each interaction
  • Avoid using company name repeatedly during the call

Language Safeguards

DO

  • Use company name only at beginning of call
  • Reference specific company plans
  • Focus on the customer's specific needs

DON'T

  • Say "we also offer..." (implies multiple companies)
  • Mention policies of other providers
  • Use generic "our company" phrasing
Unsafe Language:

"Our company policies are similar across all our energy brands."

Safe Language:

"Your specific plan includes..."

If Misbranding Occurs

  1. Don't draw attention to the mistake
  2. Avoid explicitly correcting yourself if customer hasn't noticed
  3. After the call, report the incident to your supervisor for coaching

Summer Bill Management

Summer months see a 30-50% increase in average energy bills due to cooling demands!

Why Bills Increase

  • Air conditioning runs longer and more frequently
  • Peak demand pricing during summer months
  • Additional cooling appliances in use
  • Longer daylight hours change usage patterns

Customer Communication

Recommended Script

"I understand your concern about the bill increase. During summer months, cooling costs typically account for up to 50% of energy usage. The recent temperature increase has impacted many customers similarly."

DO

  • Show empathy
  • Cite specific temperature data
  • Offer usage comparisons

DON'T

  • Use technical jargon
  • Sound dismissive
  • Rush the explanation

Customer Scenarios

"This bill is ridiculous! I want to speak to a manager right now!"

"I understand your frustration about this bill. I'd feel the same way. Let me look at your account and see what options we have to address this high bill together."

Key Techniques:

  • Acknowledge emotion without getting defensive
  • Avoid using customer's name per policy
  • Offer immediate action

"I don't understand why my bill doubled. This makes no sense!"

"I can help explain what happened. Your bill shows your AC usage increased by 45% compared to last month, which matches the temperature increase we saw in the area. Let me walk you through the details."

Key Techniques:

  • Provide clear, simple explanations
  • Reference specific account data
  • Offer to break down the bill step-by-step

"I've asked three times already. I want to speak to your supervisor now!"

"I understand you'd like to speak with a supervisor. I'd be happy to transfer you to our dedicated escalation team at ASG who can address your concerns immediately."

"I'll transfer you to our US BASED ASG team."

Key Techniques:

  • Acknowledge the request without promising specifics
  • Transfer only to ASG
  • Never mention or transfer to US BASED ASG
